Chapter 1 — Introduction

Why Paladin / Monk / Shaman is S-Tier

A complete self-sufficient unit: Shaman Slow defangs every melee mob, Paladin tanks + controls + wrecks undead, Monk deletes the slowed target. You don't need a group — you need patience. The trio dominates long, controlled fights.

Strengths
  • ★★★★★ vs single bosses (Slow + sustain + steady DPS)
  • Anti-undead (Paladin) — huge in Befallen/Najena/Guk/Unrest
  • Best long farming sessions in the game
  • Self-heal (Shaman + Lay on Hands + Mend)
Weaknesses
  • No Complete Heal (no Cleric) — emergency = Shaman big heal + LoH
  • Multi-adds need Root/Stun (no mez/charm)
  • Gear-dependent early; slow AoE clear
  • Caster mobs (necros) hit above their con

Difficulty 4 strategy

D4 = more XP, better loot, more merge duplicates. Rule: control every pull, never wipe. Slow first, tank stable, then full DPS. Bad approach = pull everything. Your trio wins by attrition, not aggression.

Recommended race

Human = safest all-round (balanced stats, works cleanly with all three). Iksar = best natural regen + AC (great for Monk/Shaman) but confirm Paladin/deity alignment is allowed for Iksar in EQL. Ogre/Troll = huge HP/STA + regen for a tanky Shaman-lead, frontal stun immunity (Ogre) — strong but ugly models. EQL: race no longer hard-restricts class the classic way, but deity/primary lock at 11 — verify on Discord.

Stat priorities

Haste → Weapons → STR → DEX → HP
Shaman
WIS → Mana → HP → Resists

🔁 Named Farm Loops — how to farm intelligently

🔍 On the maps below: mouse wheel = zoom · drag = pan · double-click = reset.
The EverQuest way: don't sit and wait on one spawn. Clear the named in a fixed loop; by the time you circle back to the start, they've respawned. Revamped zones (Befallen/Najena/Blackburrow) have guaranteed named, no placeholders → every loop pays. Refill mana while walking (Shaman Cannibalize) so there's zero downtime.

Chaining rules

  • Loop length ≈ respawn. Move so a full circuit takes roughly as long as the named take to repop (~8-22 min). Too fast = you wait; too slow = you leave loot on the ground.
  • Slow → tank → burst every pull. Don't skip Slow to "save time" — a wipe costs more than a slow.
  • No downtime: Shaman Cannibalize + med while walking to the next named; pull the next as soon as mana's up.
  • Priority: hit the S named every loop (Tenderizer, Baron Telyx, Frenzied Ghoul, Ghoul Lord); A/B only if up and on the way.
  • Exact EQL respawns are mostly undocumented — time your first few loops in-game and adjust.

Chapter 3 — Dungeon Atlas

Step-by-step directions — landmark by landmark, so you know exactly where each named/key/secret is without me redrawing the map. Keep a real map (Brewall in-game, or EQ Atlas) open beside it; the /loc numbers pin each spot.
🫥 Navigate with invisibility (key skill): your Invisibility to Undead (IVU) — lvl 17 — lets you walk past undead unseen — huge for reaching a named/camp in Befallen, Najena, Unrest, Guk dead side without grinding through every mob. Normal Invisibility (vs the living) — lvl 27 — does the same vs living mobs. ⚠️ Travel only — invis breaks the moment you attack, and some mobs see through it (Lower Guk vampire bats see invis; higher-level / see-invis undead ignore IVU; casters can ignore it). Move slowly, don't bump aggro.
Befallen (map ref: EQ Atlas ↗)
  1. Entrance: you arrive at the top of a staircase; the main hall below holds ~4 skeletons all in each other's aggro range. Pull singly back up the stairs — don't wade in. This hall is your Pristine Leather/Chain + magic-weapon farm.
  2. The well = your hub (centre of floor 1). The room just WEST of the well = Skeleton Lrodd + the Dark Elf SK who drops the Splintered Wooden Key (door 1) -334,183. The pit room (female SK) = Charred Bone Key (door 2) -552,-54. The Ogre SK (also drops Barbed Armplates) = Smoked Glass Key (door 3) -511,152.
  3. Drop down the well → floors 2/3. First right after you land = Priest Amiaz -494,95. Down here: Baron Telyx, Cmdr Windstream, Thaumaturgist. Troll SK (any key + Bone Bladed Claymore) -372,-105.
  4. Danger: floor-3 necromancers cast while running and hit above con — kill them first. Never sit/loot mid-room (it widens aggro radius → swarm). Gynok Moltor (rare, Ring of Opolla) spawns by the WC zone-line -80,-45.
  5. Escape: bind at the entrance. The entry tunnel is long — if a train is in it, wait it out, don't chase.
Najena (map ref: EQ Atlas ↗)
  1. Front rooms: each packs ~4 skeletons + earth elementals + ogre guards, all aggro. Below ~17, farm only these front rooms and pull to the door.
  2. The key chain IS the route (in order): find Bonecracker (in a room with 2 dark-elf necros) → dull bone key. Kill the Ogre Captain → shiny metal key. Keep both — they're the only non-No-Rent keys. (Patch Jul 7: locked doors now auto-search your bags for keys — no need to hold a key on the cursor.)
  3. Shiny metal key opens the cell outside Rathyl; dull bone key opens Rathyl's area — you need both to reach him. Rathyl → bloodstained key + Traveller's Pouch (-25% wt).
  4. Bloodstained key → Drelzna's room. Drelzna → golden crescent key → opens Najena's room via the underwater tunnel. The Widow Mistress (Stiletto) is by the spiders beyond that underwater tunnel.
  5. The Tenderizer → Kitchen Toolbelt (haste) (also drops the fast 1H-blunt "The Tenderizer" weapon) — hit it every pass, your monk priority.
  6. Unbound Flame — fire elemental, L25, -26,360, respawn 5 min, Stun/Root → Flaming Fist (monk H2H, +20 fire). Kill it fast, it's a caster.
  7. Najena herself (deep, via the underwater tunnel) → Clawed Knuckle Ring (~10%) + Flowing Black Robe.
  8. Secret/danger: 2 pit traps on the upper level drop you into the prison. Do NOT attack Moosh in the prison — it wrecks Rivervale faction.
Estate of Unrest (map ref: EQ Atlas ↗)
  1. Yard (10-16): death beetles + tormented dead roam — fight along the back & west walls. Lesser Blade Fiend (→ Gladius) 699,90 is in the 1st-floor back room.
  2. Key chain (launch order) — 1st: the first named, the Hand (1st floor), drops the first key → opens the 1st-floor locked door.
  3. 2nd: through it → Reclusive Ghoul Magus on the balcony 700,27 (→ Savant's Cap) drops the balcony key.
  4. 3rd: balcony key → up to the 2nd floor, opens the Barman / undead barkeep room → he drops the next key (for the 4th floor).
  5. 4th floor: that key → Undead Knight of Unrest 697,-76 (→ Bloodstained Tunic; Harm Touch + Fear). On the opposite side of the 4th floor, the Priest of Najena drops the library key.
  6. Garanel (basement): the library key opens the library door → the book inside actuates the mechanism that opens the way down → Garanel Rucksif 485,147 → Dwarven Work Boots + Jagged Band. ⚠️ Mortuary Fungus (drowsy) + fake-lava trap on the way.
  7. Escape: Hand Room → balcony → patio roof = straight shot to the zone line. Bind at the entrance.
Lower Guk (map ref: dead ↗ / live ↗)
  1. Getting in: from Upper Guk, three entrances drop near Dead side (two need a short swim, the dry one is closest to the front entrance). The single Live-side entrance is the deepest into Upper Guk.
  2. Dead side (your undead farm): chain Frenzied Ghoul (FBSS) → Ghoul Lord (1558,-749) (Sword of Ykesha — cul-de-sac NE of the Jail Room) → King → Executioner → Assassin → Magi, then loop.
  3. Danger: vampire bats see through invis (dead side); on Live side the Huge Water Elemental sees invis and nearby frogloks assist it; caster frogloks (shaman/wizard) — kill first, they run.
  4. Escape: Dead side is far from every exit — always carry an evac. The King's Room on Live side has a back zone-exit to Upper Guk (but it runs you past the toughest mob, emergency use only).

Chapter 6 — Trio Best in Slot (ONE character)

It's one body, one set of slots — not three characters. EQL is a solo game (that's exactly why one character carries 3 classes) → BiS = survive + self-sustain + haste, not group roles; aggro/hate stats are wasted solo (nobody to hold aggro from). Priority per slot: AC + HP first (you tank in D4) → one Haste item (biggest DPS jump) → keep some WIS (Shaman mana). Plate everywhere for AC.
Guide-defining rule: a BiS you can grab at a low level = priority #1 — it lasts for ages. Earliest priority pickups: Befallen ~12 (Barbed arms/legs, Antiqued Silver Band), Najena ~20 (Kitchen Toolbelt = haste), Upper Guk ~30 (Ghoulbane). Grab those the moment you're the right level.

🔺 Haste cap & stacking: total haste caps at ~80% before lvl 50, 100% at 50+. Three separate categories that stack together: worn/item (your belt) + spell (Quickness) + Monk discipline (~20%). Within a category only the highest counts → wear one haste belt (FBSS 21% > Kitchen Toolbelt 10%); a 2nd haste item does nothing. So: keep FBSS AND cast Quickness (they add), don't drop one for the other, and don't waste a slot on a second haste item. FBSS 21% + Quickness + Monk disc nears the ~80% cap — anything past the cap is wasted.
⚔️ Weapon choice in the field (solo, ~28+): Default = Ghoulbane + shield in undead zones — its Dismiss Undead proc (140-162) nearly matches dual-fist DPS vs undead, and you keep the shield's AC/block (huge when you're your own healer). Switch to 2× Flaming Fist when: (a) the mob is living (Ghoulbane's proc does nothing → fists win DPS), or (b) the fight is safe — Slow landed, small pull — for max kill speed. Trigger = your Slow: not-yet-slowed / risky = Ghoulbane+shield; slowed & under control = dual fists.

Chapter 7 — Trio Strategy (ONE character, 3 class kits)

Read this first: you are one character with three class toolkits. When this guide says "Monk pulls → Shaman slows → Paladin tanks", that's you using your Monk pull, then casting your Shaman Slow, then holding aggro with Paladin — all on the same body, in sequence. There is no group. Your limit is your own global cooldowns and mana, so act in the right order, not all at once.
🎛️ Build @ ~28 (solo undead farm) — stances · skills · spell bar

Stances & Invocations (Actions [L]; drain endurance/stamina)

Key rule: 1 Stance + 1 Invocation active at the same time — pick one of each for the situation.

Your STANCES (Pal/Monk/Sha): Offensive (+100% melee dmg, +25% hit) · Striker (weapon-skill hits ×3, +25% hit — if you spam kicks) · Defensive (−50% phys / −20% magic) · Mage Hunter (−50% magic / −20% phys) · Evasive (95% evade, monk) · Balanced (−10% all dmg, +endurance regen).

Your INVOCATIONS: Unyielding (HP regen ×2 + 25% resist Fear/mez/charm — huge vs the Undead Knight's Fear) · OverChannel (−150 resist → your Slow/DoTs LAND) · SpellBlade (gem-1 spell becomes a melee proc — put Stun/nuke there) · Recovery (mana regen ×2) · Divine (spending mana also heals you) · Empowering (+spell dmg & heal power).

Best combos @28 (undead): ① Offensive + Unyielding = default burst (dmg + HP regen + fear resist) · ② Mage Hunter/Defensive + OverChannel = caster rooms / when Slow won't stick · ③ Offensive + SpellBlade (gem-1 = Stun) = melee that procs spells · ④ Balanced + Recovery = mana-starved. Watch the yellow stamina bar.

Skills (level by using — keep maxed)

Defense · Dodge · Parry · Riposte · Block (shield) · Offense · Dual Wield · Hand-to-Hand + 1H Slashing (Ghoulbane) · Bind Wound. Abilities: Mend + Feign Death (Monk), Lay on Hands (Paladin).

Spell bar — 8 gems

  1. Slow (Sha) — the fight
  2. Heal (Sha)
  3. Regen / HoT (Sha) — sustain
  4. Cannibalize (Sha) — HP→mana
  5. Malaise (Sha) — lands Slow/DoT + debuff
  6. Dismiss Undead / undead nuke (Pal) — DPS vs undead
  7. Stun (Pal) — interrupt necros + burst
  8. DoT (Sha) — extra DPS (or Root for 2-3 pulls)

Cast before the fight, then swap out of the bar: SoW · STR/STA + AC buff · IVU (navigation) · Cure. Exact EQL spell names/levels — confirm in-game or eqlwiki "Stances & Invocations" / "Sample Build".

Pulling
  • Monk pulls 1 by line-of-sight; FD-split at 17+.
  • Pull to a safe corner, back to a wall.
Crowd control
  • Shaman Root extras; Paladin stun (undead); Monk FD to reset a bad pull.
Slow rotation
  • Slow the instant you engage, before DPS. Re-slow if it wears.
Healing
  • Shaman heals main; Mend on cooldown.
Emergency rotation
  • Shaman biggest heal → Lay on Hands → FD (Monk) if it's collapsing. (No Complete Heal — no Cleric.)
Mana management
  • Cannibalize between pulls; med only when needed; SoW to reposition.
Multi-pulls & boss
  • 2-3: Root extras, kill slowed one first.
  • Boss: Slow → tank stable → Monk full DPS behind → save LoH for the spike. Long fight = your win condition.
